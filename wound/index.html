Tetanus Vaccination History
Prior immunization status and time since last booster
Vaccine products
  • Tdap (Adacel, Boostrix) — preferred if never received
  • Td — if Tdap previously given or contraindicated
  • TIG (HyperTET) — 250 units IM, separate site from vaccine
  • If both needed: administer at separate sites simultaneously
Tetanus-prone features
  • Contaminated (soil, saliva, feces)
  • Puncture wounds, crush injuries, avulsions
  • Animal or human bites
  • Burns or frostbite
  • Wounds >6 hours old
  • Devitalized or ischemic tissue
Recommendation
Based on ACIP tetanus prophylaxis recommendations. Tdap/Td: 0.5 mL IM deltoid. TIG: 250 units IM contralateral site. Not medical advice.
1
Wound Characteristics
Degree of contamination, tissue viability, and infection risk
Closure methods
  • Sutures: Nylon or Prolene for skin; Vicryl 3-0/4-0 for deep layers
  • Staples: Scalp, trunk, extremities — fast, equivalent outcomes
  • Tissue adhesive (Dermabond): Superficial, low-tension; avoid hands, joints, mucosa
  • Steri-strips: Low-tension edges only; not over joints
Suture removal timing
  • Face: 3–5 days
  • Scalp: 7–10 days
  • Neck: 5–7 days
  • Chest / abdomen: 10–14 days
  • Back: 10–14 days
  • Extremity: 10–14 days
  • Over joint: 14 days
  • Hand / foot: 10–14 days
Closure Approach
Based on ACEP, IDSA, and ACS wound management guidelines. Irrigate all wounds with normal saline or tap water at adequate volume. Not medical advice.
1
Biting Species
Species determines rabies risk category and prophylaxis approach
Rabies PEP regimen (if indicated)
  • Unvaccinated: HRIG 20 IU/kg on day 0 (infiltrate wound site; remainder IM) + vaccine days 0, 3, 7, 14
  • Previously vaccinated against rabies: No HRIG. Vaccine days 0 and 3 only.
  • Irrigate wound with soap and water, then povidone-iodine before PEP
Antibiotic prophylaxis
  • Cat bites: All bites — Amox-clav 875/125 mg BID × 5 days
  • Dog bites: Selective — puncture wounds, hands/face/feet, immunocompromised — Amox-clav 875/125 mg BID × 5 days
  • Human bites: Clenched-fist and through-skin wounds — Amox-clav 875/125 mg BID × 5–7 days
  • PCN allergy: Moxifloxacin 400 mg daily × 5 days, or doxycycline 100 mg BID + metronidazole 500 mg TID
Rabies Assessment
Based on ACIP rabies PEP guidelines (MMWR) and IDSA bite wound recommendations. Always consult local/state public health for rabies exposures. Not medical advice.
