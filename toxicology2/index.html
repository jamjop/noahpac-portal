Toxin → antidote quick reference. ↻ Reviewed quarterly

Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 — available 24/7 nationwide. Call early, especially for unfamiliar ingestions, pediatric exposures, or when the antidote/dosing below doesn't clearly fit the clinical picture. Supportive care (airway, breathing, circulation) always takes priority over antidote administration.

Beta-blockers

Up to 10 mg bolus, then infusion 2–5 mg/hr. High-dose insulin/euglycemia therapy (HIET) for refractory shock — regular insulin 1 unit/kg bolus, then 0.5–1 unit/kg/hr with dextrose to maintain euglycemia

Acetaminophen — Rumack-Matthew Nomogram Guidance

Single acute ingestion, known time
Draw APAP level at 4h post-ingestion (or later if ingestion time uncertain — never interpret a level drawn before 4h). Plot on Rumack-Matthew nomogram; treat with NAC if above the treatment line (150 mcg/mL at 4h).
Unknown ingestion time / staggered ingestion
Nomogram does not apply. Start NAC empirically if any suggestion of a potentially toxic ingestion (e.g. >7.5g or >150 mg/kg cumulative over <24h) while awaiting levels/LFTs — do not wait for a 4h level that will never be interpretable.
Delayed presentation / established hepatotoxicity
Start NAC regardless of level if there's evidence of hepatic injury (elevated AST/ALT) or presentation is >24h post-ingestion — NAC has benefit even in late/fulminant hepatic failure. Involve transplant hepatology early for severe cases.

General Principles

GI decontamination
Activated charcoal (1 g/kg) is most beneficial within 1–2h of ingestion for appropriate toxins — not all toxins bind charcoal (e.g. iron, lithium, alcohols). Whole bowel irrigation for iron, sustained-release products, or body packers. Gastric lavage is rarely indicated today.
Delayed presentations
Some ingestions look deceptively benign early — acetaminophen, sustained-release preparations, and some plant/mushroom toxins can have a symptom-free latent period before organ injury manifests. Don't be falsely reassured by an early asymptomatic exam.
Supportive care first
Airway, breathing, circulation take priority over antidote administration in every case. Many toxidromes are managed successfully with aggressive supportive care alone even when a specific antidote isn't available or isn't yet confirmed necessary.
When in doubt
Call Poison Control (1-800-222-1222) early — for unusual exposures, pediatric ingestions, unclear dosing, or antidote availability questions. Toxicology or critical care consultation for refractory or complex cases.
